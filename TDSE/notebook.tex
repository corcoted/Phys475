    The general solution follows the method of
http://jakevdp.github.com/blog/2012/09/05/quantum-python/

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Use the potential to propagate a half time step in x space
\item
  FFT
\item
  Use the kinetic energy operator to propagate one whole time step in k
  space
\item
  IFFT
\item
  Use the potential to propagate a half time step in x space
\end{itemize}

For time varying potentials, the propagation term is exp(-i
integral(V(x),dt)/hbar). The integral can be done analytically or
numerically. Here, if numerical integration is used, we use one step of
the trapezoidal approximation. This may not work if the time step is too
large.

Hard boundaries are assumed. Be sure xmin and xmax are sufficiently far
from the region of interest for the initial state you choose.
